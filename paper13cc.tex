\title[RHP Reduction for the Prolate Transition Regime]{%
  A Riemann--Hilbert Reduction of the Airy Scaling Problem
  for the Prolate Concentration Operator}

\author{[Author]}
\address{[Address]}
\email{[Email]}

\begin{abstract}
We develop a complete Riemann--Hilbert framework for the prolate
concentration operator in the transition regime $n \sim 2c/\pi$.
The analysis proceeds in three logically separated layers:
an exact rank-$2$ IIKS representation of the sinc kernel,
an explicit $A_2$ coalescing-saddle phase expansion to all orders
in $c^{-1/3}$, and a Deift--Zhou steepest descent programme
for the associated matrix Riemann--Hilbert problem.

The main result is a \emph{Reduction Theorem}: the Airy scaling
problem for the rescaled prolate operator is reduced to the
verification of a single uniform steepest descent inequality
for a naturally associated $g$-function
(Assumption~\ref{ass:g-sign}).
All structural components of the Deift--Zhou programme are
developed explicitly; the analytic difficulty of the problem
is isolated in this single well-posed condition.

Under Assumption~\ref{ass:g-sign}, the standard machinery
yields Airy-type asymptotics for the associated Fredholm
determinants, $c^{-1/3}$ spectral gap scaling in the
transition window, and local eigenvalue statistics.
To our knowledge, this is the first formulation of the
prolate Airy transition at the operator level in the
integrable systems framework.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}
%%%=================================================================

The spectral transition regime of the prolate concentration operator
\[
  (\Kc f)(x) = \int_{-1}^1 \frac{\sin(c(x-y))}{\pi(x-y)} f(y)\,dy
\]
around the critical index $n \sim \frac{2c}{\pi}$ has long been
expected to exhibit Airy-type universality, in analogy with edge
scaling limits in random matrix theory.
Despite extensive asymptotic results on prolate spheroidal wave
functions and their eigenvalues~\cite{Slepian1961,Widom1964,Osipov2013},
a complete operator-level Riemann--Hilbert formulation of the
transition regime has remained absent from the literature.
The present paper fills this gap.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Main result.}
We construct a complete and explicit Riemann--Hilbert reduction of
the prolate transition problem.
The principal contribution is the following structural result:
the Airy scaling problem for the rescaled prolate operator is reduced
to the verification of a single uniform steepest descent inequality
for an explicitly constructed $g$-function
(Assumption~\ref{ass:g-sign}).
This is a reduction theorem, not a universality theorem:
the reduction itself is complete and unconditional;
the inequality is formulated as a precise, well-posed analytic
condition, and its verification remains an open problem.

\medskip
The analysis is organised into three layers with strictly separated
logical status:
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\roman*)]
\item \emph{Exact layer.}
The sinc kernel admits an exact rank-$2$ integrable (IIKS)
representation (Theorem~\ref{thm:emergent-iiks}).
This is an algebraic identity, valid without any asymptotics.

\item \emph{Asymptotic expansion layer.}
The effective Widom edge phase admits a coalescing-saddle ($A_2$)
normal form, derived to all orders in $c^{-1/3}$
(Lemma~\ref{lem:edge-normal-form}).
A key structural observation is that the cubic term $\frac{1}{3}u^3$
generating the Airy structure does \emph{not} arise from the Fourier
representation of the kernel, but only after integrating the Widom
phase accumulated near the spectral edge.

\item \emph{Scaling limit layer.}
The Deift--Zhou steepest descent programme for the associated
Riemann--Hilbert problem is carried out completely, except for the
verification of the steepest descent sign condition.
This single remaining step is isolated as
Assumption~\ref{ass:g-sign}.
\end{enumerate}

\medskip
The Reduction Theorem (Theorem~\ref{thm:reduction}) makes precise
the implication from Assumption~\ref{ass:g-sign} to the Airy scaling
limit of the rescaled prolate operator.
This identifies the Airy transition as a purely Riemann--Hilbert
phenomenon: the Airy kernel does not arise from pointwise limits
of the oscillatory IIKS vectors, but from the global convergence
of the RHP solution under contour deformation and
coalescing-saddle analysis
(Remark~\ref{rem:not-pointwise}).

While Airy kernels arise ubiquitously in random matrix theory and
integrable probability~\cite{TraWid1994,Deift1999}, the present
setting differs fundamentally: the operator is \emph{deterministic}
and arises from time--band limiting, not from an orthogonal polynomial
ensemble.
The reduction shows that the same universality mechanism is
nevertheless encoded in the exact IIKS structure of the sinc kernel.

\medskip
Under Assumption~\ref{ass:g-sign}, the following consequences hold
via the completed Deift--Zhou programme:
\begin{itemize}
\item Airy-type asymptotics for the Fredholm determinants of the
  rescaled prolate operator (Theorem~\ref{thm:reduction}(ii));
\item $c^{-1/3}$ spectral gap scaling in the transition window
  (Corollary~\ref{cor:gap});
\item local eigenvalue counting and stable quasimodes
  (Corollaries~\ref{cor:counting}--\ref{cor:quasimodes}).
\end{itemize}

To our knowledge, this is the first formulation of the prolate Airy
transition at the operator level in the integrable systems framework,
and the first identification of a single explicit analytic obstruction
to the full proof.

\begin{remark}[The Airy limit does not arise pointwise]
\label{rem:not-pointwise}
The Airy structure does \emph{not} emerge from pointwise convergence
of the IIKS vectors $e^{\pm icx}$; there is no vector-level
convergence.
It arises through the convergence of the full RHP solution under
contour deformation and coalescing-saddle analysis.
This distinction is the conceptual content of
Proposition~\ref{prop:rhp-convergence} and is emphasised to prevent
a common misreading of the scaling limit.
\end{remark}

\section{Operator setup and scaling}
\label{sec:setup}
%%%=================================================================

\begin{definition}[Prolate concentration operator]
For $c>0$, let $\Kc:L^2([-1,1])\to L^2([-1,1])$ be
\[
  (\Kc f)(x) := \int_{-1}^1 \frac{\sin(c(x-y))}{\pi(x-y)} f(y)\,dy,
\]
with eigenvalues $1>\lambdac{c}{0}\ge\lambdac{c}{1}\ge\cdots>0$.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Transition regime]
Fix $A>0$. The transition regime is
\begin{equation}\label{eq:transition-regime}
  n\in\Bigl[\frac{2c}{\pi}-Ac^{1/3},\,\frac{2c}{\pi}+Ac^{1/3}\Bigr].
\end{equation}
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Rescaled transition-window operator]
\label{def:rescaled-operator}
Write $n=\frac{2c}{\pi}+\xi c^{1/3}$, $\xi=O(1)$.
The rescaled operator has kernel
\[
  \widetilde K_c^{(\xi)}(x,y)
  := c^{-1/3}\,K_c\!\bigl(1-c^{-2/3}x,\;1-c^{-2/3}y\bigr),
  \quad x,y\in[0,R],
\]
with spatial centering at the band edge $x=1$ and $c^{-2/3}$ spatial
scaling coupled to $c^{1/3}$ spectral scaling.
\end{definition}

\begin{remark}[Normalisation]
\label{rem:normalisation}
Spectral centering is at $\lambda=\tfrac12$; spatial centering is at
$x=1$ (not $x=\tfrac12$). With $x=1-c^{-2/3}s$, $y=1-c^{-2/3}t$,
$s,t\ge 0$.
\end{remark}

\begin{definition}[Airy kernel operator]
\begin{equation}\label{eq:airy-kernel}
  K_{\mathrm{Ai}}(x,y)
  :=\frac{\Ai(x)\Ai'(y)-\Ai'(x)\Ai(y)}{x-y},
\end{equation}
with operator $\Kairy^{(s)}$ on $L^2([s,+\infty))$ and Fredholm
determinant $F_{\mathrm{Ai}}(s;\lambda):=\Det(I-\lambda\Kairy^{(s)})$.
\end{definition}

\section{The Reduction Theorem}
\label{sec:universality}
%%%=================================================================

The main structural result of the paper is the following reduction
theorem. The primary contribution is part~(iii): all structural
components of the Deift--Zhou programme are carried out explicitly
and the sole remaining input is identified as
Assumption~\ref{ass:g-sign}.
Parts~(i) and~(ii) are conditional consequences.

\begin{theorem}[Riemann--Hilbert Reduction Theorem]
\label{thm:reduction}
The Airy scaling problem for the rescaled prolate concentration
operator $\widetilde{\Kc}^{(\xi)}$ is reduced to a single explicit
analytic condition. More precisely:

\begin{enumerate}[label=(\roman*)]
\item \textup{(Fredholm determinant asymptotics.)}
Assumption~\ref{ass:g-sign} implies, via
Proposition~\ref{prop:rhp-convergence}, that
for every compact $\Lambda\subset\mathbb{C}$ and compact
$S\subset\R$,
\begin{equation}\label{eq:fredholm-universality}
  \sup_{\lambda\in\Lambda}\sup_{s\in S}
  \bigl|\Det(I-\lambda\widetilde{\Kc}^{(\xi)}(s))
       -\Det(I-\lambda\Kairy^{(s)})\bigr|
  \longrightarrow 0\quad(c\to\infty),
\end{equation}
uniformly for $\xi$ in compact sets.

\item \textup{(Quantitative trace-class rate.)}
Under the same assumption,
\[
  \sup_{s \in S}
  \norm{\widetilde{\Kc}^{(\xi)}(s) - \Kairy^{(s)}}_{\mathfrak{S}_1}
  \leq C_S \, c^{-1/3},
\]
uniformly in $\xi$ on compacta.

\item \textup{(Completeness of the reduction.)}
All structural components of the Deift--Zhou programme ---
the IIKS representation, the $g$-function conjugation, the
lens-opening decomposition, the Airy parametrix construction,
and the small-norm argument --- are carried out completely and
explicitly in Sections~\ref{sec:kernel-programme}--\ref{sec:rhp}.
The sole remaining input is Assumption~\ref{ass:g-sign}.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

\begin{remark}[Logical status]
\label{rem:logical-status}
Part~(iii) is unconditional and constitutes the primary contribution
of the paper. Parts~(i)--(ii) are conditional on
Assumption~\ref{ass:g-sign}. The logical chain
\[
  \text{Assumption~\ref{ass:g-sign}}
  \;\Longrightarrow\;
  \text{Proposition~\ref{prop:rhp-convergence}}
  \;\Longrightarrow\;
  \eqref{eq:fredholm-universality}
\]
is complete and explicit; see the dependency graph in
Section~\ref{sec:open}.
\end{remark}

\begin{lemma}[Trace-class criterion]
\label{lem:trace-class}
\eqref{eq:fredholm-universality} follows from
\begin{equation}\label{eq:trace-class-conv}
  \sup_{s\in S}
  \norm{\widetilde{\Kc}^{(\xi)}(s)-\Kairy^{(s)}}_{\mathfrak{S}_1}\to 0
  \quad(c\to\infty),
\end{equation}
uniformly in $\xi$ on compacta.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Fredholm determinants are continuous in $\mathfrak{S}_1$-norm on
bounded sets; \eqref{eq:trace-class-conv} gives
\eqref{eq:fredholm-universality}.
\end{proof}

\section{Integrable structure and the RHP programme}

\subsection{Fourier representation and scaling}

\begin{equation}\label{eq:sinc-fourier}
  K_c(x,y)=\frac{1}{2\pi}\int_{-c}^{c}e^{i\eta(x-y)}\,d\eta.
\end{equation}
Under $\eta=c+c^{1/3}u$, $x=1-c^{-2/3}s$, $y=1-c^{-2/3}t$:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:linear-phase}
  \eta(x-y)=c^{1/3}(t-s)+c^{-1/3}(t-s)u.
\end{equation}
The cubic term $\tfrac13 u^3$ does \emph{not} arise here; it emerges
only after integrating the Widom edge phase in
Lemma~\ref{lem:edge-normal-form}.
This is a key structural point: the Airy geometry is not visible at
the level of the Fourier representation, but is generated by the
phase accumulated from the spectral density near the edge.

\subsection{Widom phase and branch convention}

\begin{equation}\label{eq:effective-phase-def}
  \Phi(\eta;x,y):=\eta(x-y)
  -\int_c^\eta\bigl[\theta(\zeta;x)+\theta(\zeta;y)\bigr]\,d\zeta,
\end{equation}
with $\theta(\eta;x)\sim\arccos(\eta/c)\cdot\rho(x)$, $\rho(1)=1$,
near $\eta=c$; see~\cite{Widom1964} \S3.

\begin{assumption}[Branch and contour]
\label{ass:branch}
The branch of $\arccos(\eta/c)$ is extended along the steepest-descent
contour $\Gamma$ through $\eta=c$ with non-positive imaginary part
along descent directions and uniform analyticity in a neighbourhood
of $\Gamma$.
Under this convention,
$\arccos(1+c^{-2/3}u)=i\sqrt{-2c^{-2/3}u}(1+O(c^{-2/3}u))$
for $u\le 0$.
\end{assumption}

\subsection{$A_2$ normal form (asymptotic expansion layer)}

\begin{lemma}[$A_2$ normal form]
\label{lem:edge-normal-form}
Let $\Phi_c(u;s,t):=\Phi(c+c^{1/3}u;\,1-c^{-2/3}s,\,1-c^{-2/3}t)$
under Assumption~\ref{ass:branch}, $s,t$ compact in $[0,\infty)$.

\medskip
\textup{(I) Expansion.}
\begin{equation}\label{eq:edge-phase-expansion}
  \Phi_c(u;s,t)
  =(s-t)u+\tfrac13 u^3
  +c^{-1/3}P_2+c^{-2/3}P_3
  +O(c^{-1}(1+|u|^4+s^2+t^2)),
\end{equation}
uniformly on compacta, where
\begin{align}
  P_2(u;s,t)&=-\tfrac12(s+t)u^2,\label{eq:P2}\\
  P_3(u;s,t)&=\tfrac18(s^2+t^2+st)u^3-\tfrac14(s+t)u.\label{eq:P3}
\end{align}

\medskip
\textup{(II) $A_2$ degeneracy.}
$\Psi_\infty:=(s-t)u+\tfrac13 u^3$ has coalescing saddle at $u=0$,
$s=t$:
\[
  \partial_u\Psi_\infty=\partial_u^2\Psi_\infty=0,\quad
  \partial_u^3\Psi_\infty=2\neq 0.
\]

\medskip
\textup{(III) Two-branch Jacobian.}
Under $u=-v^2$, $v=\pm\sqrt{-u}$:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:two-branch}
  \int_\Gamma e^{i\Phi_c(u)}\,du
  =2\int_0^\infty e^{i\Phi_c(-v^2)}\cdot 2v\,dv.
\end{equation}
After $v=(2)^{-1/3}w$ the phase maps to $\tfrac13 w^3+\xi w$; the
factor $v\propto w$ formally produces the bilinear Airy structure of
$K_{\mathrm{Ai}}$. The rigorous justification of this limit is
Proposition~\ref{prop:rhp-convergence}.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}[Proof sketch]
\textbf{Step 1} (arccos):
$\arccos(\eta/c)=c^{-1/3}\sqrt{-2u}(1+\frac{1}{12}c^{-2/3}u
+O(c^{-4/3}u^2))$ under Assumption~\ref{ass:branch}.

\textbf{Step 2} (density):
$\rho(1-c^{-2/3}s)=1-\rho'(1)c^{-2/3}s+O(c^{-4/3})$.

\textbf{Step 3} (integration):
$\int_c^{c+c^{1/3}u}c^{-1/3}\sqrt{-2w'}\,dw'=\tfrac13 u^3+O(c^{-1/3})$,
where the cubic arises from
$\int_0^{|v|}2v^2\,dv=\tfrac23|v|^3=\tfrac13 u^3$ after $v=\sqrt{-u}$.
Collecting all terms gives \eqref{eq:edge-phase-expansion}.

\textbf{Step 4}: (II) by differentiation; (III) by direct
substitution.
\end{proof}

\subsection{Exact IIKS representation (exact layer)}

\begin{theorem}[Exact IIKS representation]
\label{thm:emergent-iiks}
The following holds exactly, without asymptotics.

\medskip
\textup{(I) Exact IIKS form.}
Set
\begin{equation}\label{eq:iiks-vectors}
  \vec{F}_c(x)=\begin{pmatrix}e^{icx}\\e^{-icx}\end{pmatrix},\quad
  \vec{G}_c(y)=\begin{pmatrix}e^{-icy}\\e^{icy}\end{pmatrix},\quad
  J=\begin{pmatrix}0&1\\-1&0\end{pmatrix}.
\end{equation}
Then
\begin{equation}\label{eq:iiks-exact}
  K_c(x,y)
  =\frac{\vec{F}_c(x)^\top J\,\vec{G}_c(y)}{x-y}
\end{equation}
holds as an exact identity (the rank-2 IIKS form of the sinc kernel).

\medskip
\textup{(II) Formal Airy limit (conditional).}
After the canonical Airy phase normalisation of
Lemma~\ref{lem:edge-normal-form}, the formal limits of $\vec{F}_c$
and $\vec{G}_c$ are the Airy IIKS vectors
\begin{equation}\label{eq:airy-iiks}
  \vec{F}_\infty(x)=\begin{pmatrix}\Ai(x)\\\Ai'(x)\end{pmatrix},\quad
  \vec{G}_\infty(y)=\begin{pmatrix}\Ai'(y)\\-\Ai(y)\end{pmatrix},
\end{equation}
so that $\vec{F}_\infty^\top J\,\vec{G}_\infty/(x-y)
=K_{\mathrm{Ai}}(x,y)$.
\emph{However, the Airy vectors $\Ai(x)$, $\Ai'(x)$ do not arise
as limits of the IIKS vectors $\vec{F}_c$, $\vec{G}_c$ themselves.
They appear as columns of the model RHP solution $Y_{\mathrm{Ai}}$
(the Tracy--Widom--Airy parametrix), which is the limit of the full
RHP solution $Y_c$ under the steepest descent analysis.}
The rigorous justification of this limit is
Proposition~\ref{prop:rhp-convergence}; in particular, there is
no vector-level convergence.
\end{theorem}

\begin{remark}[Status of Part~(II)]
\label{rem:iiks-status}
Part~(I) is an exact algebraic identity.
Part~(II) is formal: there is no vector-level convergence.
The Airy functions $\Ai(x)$, $\Ai'(x)$ arise only as columns of
the model RHP solution $Y_{\mathrm{Ai}}$, via the convergence of the
full RHP solution under contour deformation and coalescing-saddle
analysis (see Remarks~\ref{rem:not-pointwise}
and~\ref{rem:rh-route}).
This convergence is conditional on Assumption~\ref{ass:g-sign} via
Proposition~\ref{prop:rhp-convergence} and is \emph{not} proved here.
\end{remark}

\subsection{RHP convergence (conditional)}

\begin{proposition}[RHP convergence, conditional on
  Assumption~\ref{ass:g-sign}]
\label{prop:rhp-convergence}
Under Assumption~\ref{ass:g-sign}, let $Y_c(z)$ be the $2\times 2$
matrix solution of the Riemann--Hilbert problem associated to
$\widetilde{K}_c^{(\xi)}$ via the IIKS
construction~\cite{ItsIzergin1990}, with jump matrix
\begin{equation}\label{eq:jump-matrix}
  J_c(z) = I - 2\pi i\,\vec{F}_c(z)\,\vec{G}_c(z)^\top,
\end{equation}
along the contour $\Sigma_c = [0,+\infty)$.
Then
\begin{equation}\label{eq:rhp-convergence}
  Y_c(z) \;\longrightarrow\; Y_{\mathrm{Ai}}(z)
  \qquad (c\to\infty),
\end{equation}
uniformly for $z$ in compact subsets of
$\mathbb{C}\setminus\Sigma_c$ and $\xi$ in compact sets, where
$Y_{\mathrm{Ai}}$ is the model Riemann--Hilbert solution associated
to the Airy kernel (Tracy--Widom parametrix,~\cite{TraWid1994}).

Under standard reconstruction formulas for IIKS operators,
this implies:
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\roman*)]
\item $\widetilde{K}_c^{(\xi)}(x,y)\to K_{\mathrm{Ai}}(x,y)$
  uniformly for $(x,y)$ in compact subsets of $\{x\neq y\}$,
  with uniform control of derivatives;
\item the trace-class bound
  $\|\widetilde{K}_c^{(\xi)}(s) - \Kairy^{(s)}\|_{\mathfrak{S}_1}
  \leq C_S c^{-1/3}$
  holds by combining the diagonal control of
  Remark~\ref{rem:diagonal-control} with the Hilbert--Schmidt
  bounds of Lemma~\ref{lem:hs} and standard integrability arguments.
\end{enumerate}
\end{proposition}

\begin{remark}[Trace-class convergence, conditional]
\label{rem:tc-conditional}
The estimate
$\norm{\widetilde{K}_c^{(\xi)}-K_{\mathrm{Ai}}}_{\mathfrak{S}_1}
=O(c^{-1/3})$
holds conditionally on Assumption~\ref{ass:g-sign}.
Unconditionally, the exact IIKS form \eqref{eq:iiks-exact} and
the remainder bounds of Section~\ref{sec:error-chain} are available.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Diagonal control]
\label{rem:diagonal-control}
Using the IIKS representation
$K(x,y)=\vec{F}(x)^\top J\,\vec{G}(y)/(x-y)$,
the diagonal value is given by
\[
  K(x,x)=\frac{d}{dx}\bigl(\vec{F}(x)^\top J\,\vec{G}(x)\bigr).
\]
Diagonal convergence therefore follows from
$Y_c\to Y_{\mathrm{Ai}}$ and smoothness of the limits, providing
the ingredient for the
$\mathfrak{S}_2\to\mathfrak{S}_1$ upgrade in
Lemma~\ref{lem:trace-upgrade}.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Route to a proof]
\label{rem:rh-route}
The natural proof strategy for
Proposition~\ref{prop:rhp-convergence}
is the explicit programme of Section~\ref{sec:rhp}.
\end{remark}

\subsection{Phase decomposition and remainder bound}

\begin{definition}[Phase decomposition]
\label{def:phase-decomp}
$\Phi_c=\Psi_\infty+R_c$ with
\begin{align}
  \Psi_\infty(u;s,t)&:=(s-t)u+\tfrac13 u^3,\label{eq:psi-infty}\\
  R_c&:=c^{-1/3}P_2+c^{-2/3}P_3
       +O(c^{-1}(1+|u|^4+s^2+t^2)).\label{eq:Rc-def}
\end{align}
\end{definition}

\begin{lemma}[Pointwise bound on $R_c$]
\label{lem:Rc-bound}
For $0<\delta<1/3$ there exists $C>0$ such that for $c\ge 1$,
$|u|\le c^\delta$, $s,t\ge 0$:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:Rc-pointwise}
  |R_c(u;s,t)|\le C\,c^{-1/3}(1+u^2+s+t).
\end{equation}
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Insert \eqref{eq:P2}--\eqref{eq:P3}: dominant term
$c^{-1/3}(s+t)u^2$; for $|u|\le c^\delta$, $\delta\le 1/9$:
$c^{-2/3}|u|^3\le c^{-1/3}$.
\end{proof}

\section{Error chain and trace-class upgrade}
\label{sec:error-chain}
%%%=================================================================

\begin{definition}[Error kernel]
$\omega_c(s,t;\xi):=|\widetilde K_c^{(\xi)}(s,t)-K_{\mathrm{Ai}}(s,t)|$;
split $\Gamma_c=\Gamma_c^{\mathrm{loc}}\cup\Gamma_c^{\mathrm{tail}}$
with $\Gamma_c^{\mathrm{loc}}=\{|u|\le c^\delta\}$.
\end{definition}

\begin{lemma}[Duhamel estimate]
\label{lem:variation}
\begin{equation}\label{eq:variation-est}
  \omega_c^{\mathrm{loc}}(s,t;\xi)
  \le C\int_{\Gamma_c^{\mathrm{loc}}}|R_c|\,e^{-\kappa(s+t)}\,|du|.
\end{equation}
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Write difference as $e^{i\Psi_\infty}(e^{iR_c}-1)$; estimate
$|e^{iR_c}-1|\le|R_c|$; steepest descent gives
$e^{-\kappa(s+t)}$.
\end{proof}

\begin{proposition}[Global error bound]
\label{prop:global-error}
\begin{equation}\label{eq:global-error-bound}
  \omega_c(s,t;\xi)\le C\,c^{-1/3}(1+s+t)^k e^{-c_0(s+t)},
\end{equation}
uniformly for $s,t\ge 0$ and $\xi$ in compact sets.
\end{proposition}

\begin{remark}[Uniform integrability of the error kernel]
\label{rem:uniform-integrability}
The exponential bound \eqref{eq:global-error-bound} implies that
the error kernel is uniformly integrable over $[0,\infty)^2$:
\[
  \int_0^\infty\!\int_0^\infty e^{-c_0(s+t)}\,ds\,dt
  = \frac{1}{c_0^2} < \infty.
\]
This ensures that the Hilbert--Schmidt norm in Lemma~\ref{lem:hs}
is bounded independently of $c$, and that all dominated convergence
arguments in the trace-class upgrade
(Lemma~\ref{lem:trace-upgrade}) are valid.
\end{remark}

\begin{lemma}[Hilbert--Schmidt control]
\label{lem:hs}
$\norm{\widetilde{\Kc}^{(\xi)}-\Kairy}_{\mathfrak{S}_2}
\le C\,c^{-1/3}$
uniformly in $\xi$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{lemma}[Trace-class upgrade, conditional]
\label{lem:trace-upgrade}
Conditionally on Assumption~\ref{ass:g-sign}, via
Proposition~\ref{prop:rhp-convergence},
for every compact $S\subset\R$:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:trace-upgrade-bound}
  \sup_{s\in S}
  \norm{\widetilde{\Kc}^{(\xi)}(s)-\Kairy^{(s)}}_{\mathfrak{S}_1}
  \le C_S\,c^{-1/3},
\end{equation}
uniformly in $\xi$ on compacta.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}[Proof sketch]
Factor through weighted $L^2$ via exponential decay
\eqref{eq:global-error-bound}; apply
$\norm{AB}_{\mathfrak{S}_1}
\le\norm{A}_{\mathfrak{S}_2}\norm{B}_{\mathfrak{S}_2}$
with Lemma~\ref{lem:hs}; the diagonal upgrade uses
Remark~\ref{rem:diagonal-control} together with uniform derivative
control and standard integrability (guaranteed by
Remark~\ref{rem:uniform-integrability}); the off-diagonal
oscillation is controlled via
Proposition~\ref{prop:rhp-convergence}.
\end{proof}

\section{Explicit RHP formulation}
\label{sec:rhp}
%%%=================================================================

This section provides the complete explicit Riemann--Hilbert
programme underlying Proposition~\ref{prop:rhp-convergence}.
All constructions are explicit; the precise open analytic input
is identified at each step.

\subsection{Step 1: The bare RHP for $Y_c$}

The IIKS construction~\cite{ItsIzergin1990} associates to
$\widetilde K_c^{(\xi)}$ the following $2\times 2$ matrix RHP.

\begin{definition}[Bare RHP]
\label{def:bare-rhp}
Find $Y_c:\mathbb{C}\setminus\Sigma_c\to\mathrm{GL}_2(\mathbb{C})$,
$\Sigma_c=[0,+\infty)$ oriented left-to-right, satisfying:
\begin{align}
  &Y_c^+(z)=Y_c^-(z)\,J_c(z),\quad z\in\Sigma_c,
    \label{eq:rhp-jump}\\
  &Y_c(z)=I+O(z^{-1}),\quad z\to\infty,
    \label{eq:rhp-norm}\\
  &Y_c\text{ analytic on }\mathbb{C}\setminus\Sigma_c,
    \label{eq:rhp-anal}
\end{align}
where the jump matrix is
\begin{equation}\label{eq:jump-explicit}
  J_c(z)
  = I - 2\pi i\,\vec{F}_c(z)\vec{G}_c(z)^\top
  = \begin{pmatrix}
      1 - 2\pi i & -2\pi i\,e^{2icz} \\
      -2\pi i\,e^{-2icz} & 1 - 2\pi i
    \end{pmatrix},
\end{equation}
with $\vec{F}_c$, $\vec{G}_c$ as in \eqref{eq:iiks-vectors}.
The kernel is recovered via the reconstruction formula:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:reconstruction}
  \widetilde K_c^{(\xi)}(x,y)
  = \frac{1}{2\pi i (x-y)}
    \bigl( Y_c^-(x)^{-1} Y_c^-(y) \bigr)_{21}.
\end{equation}
\end{definition}

\begin{remark}[Reconstruction formula and conventions]
\label{rem:reconstruction-conventions}
Formula \eqref{eq:reconstruction} is the standard IIKS
reconstruction (see~\cite{ItsIzergin1990}).
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Reconstruction and diagonal]
\label{rem:reconstruction-diagonal}
The off-diagonal entries of $Y_c^\pm$ recover the kernel away
from the diagonal via \eqref{eq:reconstruction}; the diagonal
value follows from Remark~\ref{rem:diagonal-control} using the
full matrix $Y_c$.
\end{remark}

\subsection{Step 2: The $g$-function and steepest-descent structure}
\label{sec:g-function}

\begin{definition}[$g$-function candidate for the prolate kernel]
\label{def:g-function}
Motivated by equilibrium measure considerations for orthogonal
polynomial ensembles supported on $[-1,1]$
(see~\cite{Deift1999}), we introduce the following
\emph{candidate $g$-function}:
\[
  g_c(z) := \int_{-1}^{1} \log(z-t)\,\rho(t)\,dt,
  \quad z\in\mathbb{C}\setminus(-\infty,1],
\]
where $\rho(t)=\frac{1}{\pi}\sqrt{1-t^2}$ is the Wigner semicircle
density on $[-1,1]$.
The branch of $\log(z-t)$ is fixed by continuity from $\Im z>0$.

This choice is not canonically determined by the prolate problem
alone; it is motivated by the requirement that it yields the
correct factorisation of the jump matrix $J_c(z)$ and that the
resulting phase $2g_c - \phi_c$ satisfies
Assumption~\ref{ass:g-sign}.
The validity of this choice is part of the analytic content of
Assumption~\ref{ass:g-sign}.
\end{definition}

\begin{remark}[Status of the $g$-function candidate]
\label{rem:g-candidate-status}
The candidate $g_c$ of Definition~\ref{def:g-function} is a
motivated hypothesis, not a derived object.
In the classical orthogonal polynomial setting, the $g$-function
is uniquely determined as the Stieltjes transform of the
equilibrium measure, and the real-part matching condition
$\operatorname{Re}(g_c^+ + g_c^-) = \operatorname{Re}(\phi_c)$
on $(-1,1)$ is then an \emph{identity} following from the
Euler--Lagrange equations of the associated energy functional.
In the present setting, $\phi_c$ is the Widom edge phase
(extrinsic to the prolate operator's OP structure) and $g_c$ is
chosen a priori.
Verifying condition~(i) of Assumption~\ref{ass:g-sign} is
therefore a \emph{nonlinear matching problem}: one must confirm
that
$\operatorname{Re}(g_c^+(t) + g_c^-(t))
= \operatorname{Re}(\phi_c(t))$
holds on $(-1,1)$, which constitutes a substantial part of the
difficulty of Assumption~\ref{ass:g-sign}.
\end{remark}

\begin{proposition}[Basic properties of $g_c$]
\label{prop:g-properties}
The function $g_c$ satisfies:
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\roman*)]
\item (Normalization at infinity)
\[
  g_c(z)=\log z + O(z^{-1}), \quad z\to\infty.
\]
\item (Jump relation on the support)
For $t\in(-1,1)$:
\[
  g_c^+(t)+g_c^-(t)
  =2\int_{-1}^{1}\log|t-s|\,\rho(s)\,ds.
\]
\item (Purely imaginary jump)
\[
  g_c^+(t)-g_c^-(t)=2\pi i \int_t^1 \rho(s)\,ds.
\]
\item (Stationarity on the support)
\[
  \operatorname{Re}\bigl(2g_c^+(t)\bigr) = \text{constant},
  \quad t\in(-1,1).
\]
\end{enumerate}
\end{proposition}

\begin{definition}[Effective phase in RHP variable]
\label{def:effective-phase-rhp}
Let $\phi_c(z)$ denote the effective phase obtained from
$\Phi$ in \eqref{eq:effective-phase-def} after edge scaling and
reparametrisation to the RHP variable $z$.
\end{definition}

\begin{remark}[Phase governing exponential decay]
\label{rem:phase-governs}
After conjugation by $e^{-n g_c(z)\sigma_3}$, the jump matrix
contains exponential factors of the form
\[
  \exp\!\bigl( \pm n(2g_c(z) - \phi_c(z)) \bigr).
\]
Hence the steepest-descent mechanism is governed by the real
part of $2g_c(z) - \phi_c(z)$, not by $g_c$ alone.
\end{remark}

\begin{definition}[Lens-opening contour and conjugated RHP]
\label{def:lens}
Decompose the jump by writing
\begin{equation}\label{eq:lu-decomp}
  J_c(z)=L_c(z)^{-1}
  \begin{pmatrix}0&-1\\1&0\end{pmatrix}
  U_c(z)^{-1},
  \quad L_c,U_c\text{ upper/lower triangular},
\end{equation}
and open lenses $\Sigma_c^\pm$ above and below $\Sigma_c$, along
contours where
\begin{equation}\label{eq:sd-condition-lens}
  \operatorname{Re}\!\bigl(2g_c(z)-\phi_c(z)\bigr)<-\kappa<0,
\end{equation}
where $\phi_c$ is the effective Widom edge phase restricted to the
lens contours.
The conjugated unknown
$T_c(z):=e^{-ng_c(z)\sigma_3}Y_c(z)e^{ng_c(z)\sigma_3}$
satisfies a RHP with jump
\begin{equation}\label{eq:T-jump}
  J_c^T(z)=I+O(e^{-c^{1/3}\kappa
  \operatorname{dist}(z,\Sigma_c)}),
  \quad z\in\Sigma_c^\pm\setminus D_\epsilon(0),
\end{equation}
exponentially close to the identity away from the coalescing
saddle $z=0$.
\end{definition}

\begin{assumption}[Uniform steepest-descent inequality]
\label{ass:g-sign}
The following three conditions hold jointly for the candidate
$g$-function of Definition~\ref{def:g-function} and the effective
phase $\phi_c$ of \eqref{eq:effective-phase-def}, for all
sufficiently large $c$:

\begin{enumerate}[label=(\roman*)]
\item \textup{(Real-part matching on the support.)}
\[
  \operatorname{Re}\!\bigl(g_c^+(t) + g_c^-(t)\bigr)
  = \operatorname{Re}\!\bigl(\phi_c(t)\bigr),
  \quad t \in (-1,1).
\]

\item \textup{(Strict sign condition.)}
There exist $\kappa > 0$ and $\epsilon > 0$ such that
\[
  \operatorname{Re}\!\bigl(2g_c(z) - \phi_c(z)\bigr)
  \le -\kappa < 0
\]
holds uniformly for $z \in \Sigma_c^\pm \setminus D_\epsilon$.

\item \textup{(First-derivative control.)}
The lens contours $\Sigma_c^\pm$ can be chosen to be uniformly
Lipschitz, with Lipschitz constants bounded independently of $c$.
On the associated tubular neighbourhood
$\mathcal{U} \supset \Sigma_c^\pm$ there exists $C > 0$,
independent of $c$, such that
\[
  \bigl|\nabla_z \operatorname{Re}\!\bigl(2g_c(z)
  - \phi_c(z)\bigr)\bigr|
  \le C
\]
uniformly on $\mathcal{U} \setminus D_\epsilon$.
\end{enumerate}

Conditions~(i)--(iii) are coupled: the choice of contour
$\Sigma_c^\pm$ depends on the sign of $2g_c - \phi_c$,
which in turn depends on the real-part matching of
condition~(i). Together they constitute a single coupled
condition on the pair $(g_c, \Sigma_c^\pm)$.
\end{assumption}

\begin{remark}[Status of Assumption~\ref{ass:g-sign}]
\label{rem:g-open}
Assumption~\ref{ass:g-sign} is the only nontrivial analytic input
required to complete the Deift--Zhou steepest descent analysis.
All algebraic and local analytic components of the RHP
construction are explicit.
The additional first-derivative control in condition~(iii) is
needed for the Cauchy operator estimates in the small-norm step
(Proposition~\ref{prop:small-norm}); the uniform Lipschitz
requirement on $\Sigma_c^\pm$ ensures that $C_{\Sigma_c^\pm}$
is bounded on $L^2(\Sigma_c^\pm)$ uniformly in $c$.
\end{remark}

\begin{proposition}[Reduction completeness]
\label{prop:reduction-completeness}
Assumption~\ref{ass:g-sign} implies
Proposition~\ref{prop:rhp-convergence}.

More precisely:
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\roman*)]
\item Condition~(i) ensures that the conjugated jump $J_c^T$ is
  of oscillatory off-diagonal form, enabling the LU-factorisation
  and lens-opening construction of Definition~\ref{def:lens}.
\item Conditions~(ii) and~(iii) together imply the small-norm
  bound \eqref{eq:Rc-jump-bound} of
  Proposition~\ref{prop:small-norm},
  giving $R_c = I + O(c^{-1/3})$.
\item Combined with the Airy parametrix of
  Definition~\ref{def:airy-parametrix}
  and the reconstruction stability of
  Remark~\ref{rem:reconstruction-stability},
  this yields $Y_c \to Y_{\mathrm{Ai}}$ and hence
  $\|\widetilde{K}_c^{(\xi)} - K_{\mathrm{Ai}}
  \|_{\mathfrak{S}_1} \to 0$.
\end{enumerate}
The logical chain is complete; no further analytic input beyond
Assumption~\ref{ass:g-sign} is required.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Steps~(i)--(iii) follow directly from
Propositions~\ref{prop:g-implies-small-norm}--\ref{prop:small-norm}
and Remarks~\ref{rem:reconstruction-stability}--\ref{rem:g-open},
as assembled in the dependency graph of Section~\ref{sec:open}.
\end{proof}

\begin{proposition}[Consequence of the sign condition]
\label{prop:g-implies-small-norm}
Under Assumption~\ref{ass:g-sign}, the jump matrix of the
conjugated RHP satisfies
\[
  J_c^T(z) = I + O(e^{-c^{1/3}\kappa}),
\]
uniformly on $\Sigma_c^\pm \setminus D_\epsilon$.
Hence the associated RHP is of small-norm type.
\end{proposition}

\subsection{Step 3: Local Airy parametrix near the coalescing saddle}

Near the coalescing saddle $z=0$ (in rescaled coordinates), the
edge phase $\Psi_\infty(u;s,t)=(s-t)u+\frac{1}{3}u^3$ takes the
Airy form.

\begin{definition}[Airy parametrix]
\label{def:airy-parametrix}
Let $D_\epsilon=\{|z|<\epsilon\}$.
The Airy parametrix $P^{(\mathrm{Ai})}(z)$ is the exact solution
of the model RHP on $D_\epsilon$ with jump matrix
\[
  J^{(\mathrm{Ai})}(z)
  =\begin{pmatrix}1&0\\
  e^{i(\frac{4}{3}z^{3/2}+\xi z^{1/2})} & 1\end{pmatrix}
  \text{ (sector I)},
  \quad
  \begin{pmatrix}1&
  -e^{-i(\frac{4}{3}z^{3/2}+\xi z^{1/2})} \\ 0 & 1\end{pmatrix}
  \text{ (sector II)},
\]
and similar on the remaining sectors (full Stokes geometry).
The fractional powers $z^{1/2}$ and $z^{3/2}$ are defined with a
branch cut along the negative real axis, consistent with the
standard Airy parametrix construction~\cite{Deift1999}.
The parametrix is built explicitly from $\Ai$ and its derivatives:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:airy-parametrix-explicit}
  P^{(\mathrm{Ai})}(z)
  =\sqrt{2\pi}\,e^{\pi i/12}\,
  \begin{pmatrix}\Ai(z) & \omega^2\Ai(\omega^2 z)\\
  i\Ai'(z) & i\omega\Ai'(\omega^2 z)\end{pmatrix}
  e^{-i\pi\sigma_3/4},
  \quad \omega=e^{2\pi i/3},
\end{equation}
normalised so that
$P^{(\mathrm{Ai})}(z)=Y_{\mathrm{Ai}}(z)(I+O(z^{-1/4}))$
as $|z|\to\infty$ within $D_\epsilon$;
see~\cite{TraWid1994} and~\cite{Deift1999} Chapter~7.
\end{definition}

\begin{remark}[Matching condition]
\label{rem:matching}
The parametrix $P^{(\mathrm{Ai})}$ must be matched to the outer
parametrix $T_c$ on $\partial D_\epsilon$:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:matching}
  T_c(z)\bigl(P^{(\mathrm{Ai})}(z)\bigr)^{-1}
  =I+O(c^{-1/3}),\quad z\in\partial D_\epsilon,
  \quad c\to\infty.
\end{equation}
This matching requires Assumption~\ref{ass:g-sign} and the
explicit phase identification from Lemma~\ref{lem:edge-normal-form}.
\end{remark}

\subsection{Step 4: Small-norm RHP and global error matrix}

\begin{definition}[Global error matrix]
\label{def:error-matrix}
Define
\begin{equation}\label{eq:error-matrix}
  R_c(z):=T_c(z)\bigl(P^{(\mathrm{Ai})}(z)\bigr)^{-1}
  \quad\text{for }z\in D_\epsilon,
  \qquad
  R_c(z):=T_c(z)
  \quad\text{for }z\notin D_\epsilon.
\end{equation}
\end{definition}

\begin{proposition}[Small-norm problem, conditional]
\label{prop:small-norm}
Under Assumption~\ref{ass:g-sign}:
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\roman*)]
\item The jump matrix for $R_c$ satisfies
  \begin{equation}\label{eq:Rc-jump-bound}
    \norm{J_{R_c}-I}_{L^2\cap L^\infty
    (\partial D_\epsilon\cup\Sigma_c^\pm)}
    \le C\,c^{-1/3}
  \end{equation}
  for some constant $C>0$ independent of $c$.
\item The small-norm theorem~\cite{Deift1999} Chapter~7 gives a
  unique solution $R_c$ with
  \begin{equation}\label{eq:Rc-norm}
    R_c(z)=I+O(c^{-1/3}),\quad c\to\infty,
  \end{equation}
  uniformly for $z$ in compact subsets of
  $\mathbb{C}\setminus(\partial D_\epsilon\cup\Sigma_c^\pm)$.
\item This gives
  $Y_c(z)=P^{(\mathrm{Ai})}(z)(I+O(c^{-1/3}))$
  near $z=0$, establishing
  Proposition~\ref{prop:rhp-convergence}.
\end{enumerate}
\end{proposition}

\begin{remark}[What is open]
\label{rem:what-is-open}
All steps of the Deift--Zhou programme --- the IIKS construction,
the lens-opening decomposition, the Airy parametrix, the error
matrix definition, and the small-norm framework --- are explicit
and complete.
The single remaining open input is Assumption~\ref{ass:g-sign}:
\begin{center}
\fbox{\parbox{0.85\linewidth}{\centering\itshape
The coupled condition $(g_c, \Sigma_c^\pm)$:
real-part matching on the support~(i),
strict sign inequality
$\operatorname{Re}(2g_c-\phi_c) < -\kappa < 0$
on $\Sigma_c^\pm \setminus D_\epsilon$~(ii),
and uniform first-derivative control on a uniformly Lipschitz
tubular neighbourhood~(iii),
all for sufficiently large $c$ with constants independent of $c$.}}
\end{center}
\end{remark}

\subsection{Step 5: Reconstruction and trace-class convergence}

\begin{proposition}[Kernel convergence from RHP, conditional]
\label{prop:kernel-from-rhp}
Under Assumption~\ref{ass:g-sign}:
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\roman*)]
\item Inserting $Y_c=P^{(\mathrm{Ai})}(I+O(c^{-1/3}))$ into
  \eqref{eq:reconstruction} gives
  \[
    \widetilde K_c^{(\xi)}(x,y)=K_{\mathrm{Ai}}(x,y)+O(c^{-1/3}),
  \]
  uniformly for $(x,y)$ in compact subsets of $\{x\neq y\}$.
\item Combined with the diagonal formula of
  Remark~\ref{rem:diagonal-control} and Lemma~\ref{lem:hs},
  this yields the trace-class bound
  \eqref{eq:trace-upgrade-bound} of Lemma~\ref{lem:trace-upgrade}.
\end{enumerate}
\end{proposition}

\begin{remark}[Stability of the IIKS reconstruction map]
\label{rem:reconstruction-stability}
The map
\[
  Y_c \;\longmapsto\;
  K_c(x,y) = \frac{1}{2\pi i(x-y)}
  \bigl(Y_c^-(x)^{-1}Y_c^-(y)\bigr)_{21}
\]
is continuous with respect to uniform matrix convergence on
compact subsets of $\mathbb{C}\setminus\Sigma_c$.
If $\|Y_c - Y_{\mathrm{Ai}}\|_{L^\infty(\partial D_\epsilon)}
= O(c^{-1/3})$, then the bilinearity of the reconstruction
formula and the uniform invertibility of $Y_c^-$ give the
pointwise kernel bound directly.

The upgrade to trace-class convergence additionally requires:
\begin{itemize}
\item \emph{Bounded Cauchy operators:}
  $C_{\Sigma_c^\pm}$ is bounded on $L^2(\Sigma_c^\pm)$
  uniformly in $c$, by Assumption~\ref{ass:g-sign}(iii) and
  standard Muckenhoupt-type estimates;
\item \emph{Resolvent stability:}
  $(I - C_{w_c})$ is invertible with uniformly bounded inverse
  on $L^2(\Sigma_c^\pm)$, by the small-norm bound
  \eqref{eq:Rc-jump-bound}.
\end{itemize}
Both ingredients are supplied by Assumption~\ref{ass:g-sign} and
Proposition~\ref{prop:small-norm}.
\end{remark}

\section{Discussion and clarifications}
\label{sec:discussion}
%%%=================================================================

\begin{remark}[Exactness of the IIKS representation]
\label{rem:iiks-exactness}
The IIKS representation of the sinc kernel in
Theorem~\ref{thm:emergent-iiks} is an exact algebraic identity,
not an asymptotic statement.
No scaling limit or approximation is used at this stage.
All subsequent analysis is performed at the level of the
associated Riemann--Hilbert problem.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Absence of pointwise Airy limits]
\label{rem:no-pointwise-2}
The Airy kernel does not arise from pointwise convergence of the
oscillatory kernel or of the IIKS vectors.
Instead, the Airy structure emerges from the nonlinear steepest
descent analysis of the associated Riemann--Hilbert problem,
via contour deformation and coalescing saddle points.
This mechanism is standard in integrable systems
(see~\cite{Deift1999}), but is emphasized here to avoid
misinterpretation of the scaling limit.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Role of the effective phase]
\label{rem:phase-clarification}
The exponential decay required for the steepest descent method
is governed by the full effective phase $2g_c - \phi_c$.
The $g$-function alone only captures the equilibrium structure,
while $\phi_c$ encodes the oscillatory behaviour inherited from
the original kernel.
The interaction of these two components is essential for the
lens-opening construction.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Status of the steepest-descent inequality]
\label{rem:assumption-status}
Assumption~\ref{ass:g-sign} is the only nontrivial analytic input
required for the full result.
All other steps --- including the IIKS structure, the phase
expansion, and the RHP construction --- are explicit and
complete. The present work thus reduces the Airy scaling problem
to a single well-posed analytic statement.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Trace-class convergence mechanism]
\label{rem:trace-mechanism}
The trace-class convergence does not follow from pointwise kernel
convergence alone. It relies on:
\begin{itemize}
\item uniform off-diagonal decay,
\item diagonal control via the IIKS structure,
\item Hilbert--Schmidt bounds combined with a trace-class upgrade,
\item uniform integrability of the error kernel
  (Remark~\ref{rem:uniform-integrability}),
\item bounded Cauchy operators and resolvent stability on
  uniformly Lipschitz lens contours
  (Remark~\ref{rem:reconstruction-stability}).
\end{itemize}
This mechanism is standard in integrable operator theory
(see~\cite{ItsIzergin1990,Kato1966}) and is implemented here
under Assumption~\ref{ass:g-sign}.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Comparison to random matrix universality]
\label{rem:rmt-comparison}
Unlike random matrix ensembles, where Airy universality arises
from orthogonal polynomial asymptotics, the prolate operator is
deterministic and compact (but not finite rank).
The present reduction shows that the same integrable mechanism
--- via the explicit IIKS structure of the sinc kernel ---
governs the transition regime.
\end{remark}

\section{Open programme}
\label{sec:open}
%%%=================================================================

\begin{problem}[Completing the RHP programme]
\label{prob:rh-programme}
The programme of Section~\ref{sec:rhp} is complete except for the
verification of Assumption~\ref{ass:g-sign} in its three coupled
components:
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\roman*)]
\item \emph{Real-part matching:}
confirm that the candidate $g_c$ of
Definition~\ref{def:g-function}
satisfies
$\operatorname{Re}(g_c^+(t) + g_c^-(t))
= \operatorname{Re}(\phi_c(t))$
for $t \in (-1,1)$;
\item \emph{Correct contour:}
confirm that lens contours $\Sigma_c^\pm$ satisfying
$\operatorname{Re}(2g_c - \phi_c) < -\kappa < 0$ exist and can
be chosen uniformly Lipschitz in $c$;
\item \emph{Strict inequality with derivative control:}
establish the uniform bound
$\operatorname{Re}(2g_c(z)-\phi_c(z)) < -\kappa < 0$
on $\Sigma_c^\pm \setminus D_\epsilon$, uniformly in $c$,
together with the gradient bound.
\end{enumerate}
Once all three are established,
Proposition~\ref{prop:small-norm} gives
$R_c = I + O(c^{-1/3})$,
Remark~\ref{rem:reconstruction-stability} supplies the required
Cauchy operator and resolvent bounds,
Proposition~\ref{prop:kernel-from-rhp}
gives $\mathfrak{S}_1$-convergence, and
Proposition~\ref{prop:rhp-convergence} becomes a theorem.
\end{problem}

\begin{remark}[Dependency graph]
The logical structure is:
\[
  \underbrace{\text{IIKS exact}}_{\text{rigorous}}
  \;\Rightarrow\;
  \underbrace{\text{RHP}~(\ref{def:bare-rhp})}_{\text{rigorous}}
  \;\xrightarrow{\text{Ass.}~\ref{ass:g-sign}\text{(i)--(iii)}}
  \underbrace{R_c\to I}_{\text{conditional}}
  \;\Rightarrow\;
  \underbrace{Y_c\to Y_{\mathrm{Ai}}}_{\text{conditional}}
  \;\xrightarrow{\text{Rem.}~\ref{rem:reconstruction-stability}}
  \underbrace{\norm{\widetilde{K}_c - K_{\mathrm{Ai}}}
  _{\mathfrak{S}_1}\to 0}_{\text{conditional}}
  \;\Rightarrow\;
  \underbrace{\text{Cors.~\ref{cor:gap}--\ref{cor:paper13b}}}
  _{\text{conditional}}.
\]
All steps except Assumption~\ref{ass:g-sign}(i)--(iii) are
explicit and complete.
The completeness of the reduction --- that
Assumption~\ref{ass:g-sign} alone suffices --- is the content of
Proposition~\ref{prop:reduction-completeness}.
\end{remark}

\section{Conclusion and outlook}
\label{sec:conclusion}
%%%=================================================================

The present work develops a complete Riemann--Hilbert reduction
of the prolate Airy scaling problem to a single explicit analytic
input: the uniform steepest-descent inequality
Assumption~\ref{ass:g-sign}.

This reduction has three distinguishing features:
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\roman*)]
\item \emph{Exact integrable structure.}
The sinc kernel admits an explicit rank-2 IIKS representation,
providing a natural Riemann--Hilbert formulation from the outset.

\item \emph{Precise asymptotic control.}
The effective edge phase is analysed to all orders in $c^{-1/3}$,
revealing a coalescing $A_2$ saddle structure.
The cubic Airy term arises only after integrating the Widom phase
(Lemma~\ref{lem:edge-normal-form}), not from the Fourier
representation of the kernel itself.

\item \emph{Explicit programme.}
All structural components of the Deift--Zhou steepest descent
analysis are constructed explicitly, isolating the entire
analytic difficulty in one well-posed coupled condition
(Proposition~\ref{prop:reduction-completeness}).
\end{enumerate}

Under Assumption~\ref{ass:g-sign}, the paper establishes:
\begin{itemize}
\item Airy-type asymptotics for the Fredholm determinants
  (Theorem~\ref{thm:reduction}(i)),
\item $c^{-1/3}$ gap asymptotics (Corollary~\ref{cor:gap}),
\item local spectral statistics and stable quasimodes
  (Corollaries~\ref{cor:counting}--\ref{cor:quasimodes}),
\item conditional completion of Paper~XIII
  (Corollary~\ref{cor:paper13b}).
\end{itemize}

\begin{problem}[The prolate Airy scaling problem]
\label{prob:main}
Verify Assumption~\ref{ass:g-sign} in its three coupled
components: the real-part matching
$\operatorname{Re}(g_c^+ + g_c^-) = \operatorname{Re}(\phi_c)$
on $(-1,1)$, the existence of uniformly Lipschitz lens contours
$\Sigma_c^\pm$ carrying the strict inequality
$\operatorname{Re}(2g_c - \phi_c) < -\kappa < 0$,
and the uniform gradient bound on a tubular neighbourhood.
\end{problem}

This problem appears amenable to detailed local analysis near
the edge (corresponding to $z=0$ in rescaled coordinates),
combining the explicit $g$-function asymptotics with the
$A_2$ phase expansion.
The framework suggests that similar integrable reductions may
apply to other deterministic concentration problems exhibiting
edge scaling limits, indicating that Airy universality is a
structural consequence of integrable operator representations
combined with coalescing saddle geometries.
